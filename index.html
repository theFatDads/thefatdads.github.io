Understand the Impacts and Seek Alternatives

Opioids are a group of natural or synthetic drugs used by individuals for pain relief When used as prescribed, opioids are extremely effective. However, even when they are taken as prescribed, there is extreme potential for addiction.

Repeated use can lead to physical dependence in just a matter of weeks, and psychological dependence in only a matter of days. Therefore, The Next Step encourages those in need of painkillers to search for other options. Over the counter alternatives as well as non-drug treatments that can be used for pain control that are readily available. These alternatives are much safer and more accessible. Prescribed opioids can be used in dire situations, but extreme caution should be taken.

Dispose of extra opioids

If you are done using your prescription, you should dispose of these drugs immediately. Proper disposal is crucial. These drugs should be dropped off at one of many different disposal locations. By not disposing of them, you put both yourself and family at risk. This reduces the chance that you or your loved ones accidentally take the drug or intentionally misuse the drug. If necessary, it is also acceptable to flush opioids down a toilet to dispose of them.

Seek Help.

There are a number of different signs and symptoms to look for in individuals experiencing opioid abuse or withdrawal. If you are experiencing these symptoms yourself or see them in a loved one, seek help immediately. Symptoms of abuse can be both physical, behavioral, or psychological. They include (but are not limited to) drowsiness, shallow or slow breathing, nausea, vomiting, constipation, physical agitation, poor decision making, slurred speech, euphoria, irritability, lowered motivation, anxiety attacks, depression. Symptoms could be signs of abuse of opioids.

If opioid abuse continues in a loved one for weeks after they have finished their prescription, they are at risk of physical addiction. Withdrawl symptoms include nausea, chills, diarrhea, shaking pain, depression, insomnia, fatigue. If you are experiencing withdrawals, work with your doctor to lower your dose over time, or seek help from a care facility.
